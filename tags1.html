Heading tags

h1 Hello World!

h2 Hello World!

h3 Hello World!

h4 Hello World!

h5 Hello World!
h6 Hello World!

Note: h1 defines the most important heading. h6 defines the least important heading. Only use one h1 per page - this should represent the main heading/subject for the whole page.

Bold tag:- Bold tag is intended to draw attention to the text.

Strong tag:- Strong tag also highlights the text semantically and indicates that this is an important word or section of text.

Emphasis tag:- The em tag is used to draw the reader's attention to the text and suggests that the enclosed text should be spoken with a higher pitch and with a slightly drawn out and more emphasized voice compared to a regular sentence

Italic tag:- i tag, on the other hand, is used to render text in an italicized font.

Note: The strong and em tags in HTML are used to give text more emphasis, while the i and b tags are used to italicize and bold text, respectively. However, screen readers do not interpret the b and i tags, so they will not be read aloud to users who are blind or visually impaired. Instead, they should use the strong and em tags to give text more emphasis.
